Hi everybody in this private forum!


I am very honered that I can be a part of this very exclusive VB-decompiler-group. Also because at the time of writing I'm not working on my UFO-project till December and my homepage is more down than up, and still I was asked to be a member here, thanks.

Since this is a private forum, I could tell some details about myself. My real name is Erwin Lips, not Justin Sane, that's just a name I stole from a guy at Napster 2 years ago. I'm 19 years old (19 august 1983) and live in a small village in The Netherlands (in Europe).

Of cource I like computers (like all of us). I started when I was 13 by playing games in DOS. And at the time I was ready to learn DOS-batch-files, I discovered QuickBasic. The Dutch version of QB came with DOS, so I could learn the Basic-language in my own language. Later got the english verion with the compiler in it. Much later (2.5 years ago), I got the illegal version of VB5. Made some stupid programs for a year and 1.5 years ago I started a Virus Wizard, just a stupid program that created a fake virus (more a worm) based on the user input. I got some bad comments on it, saying that it was not a real virus. So I planned to make a virus that adds itself to PE-executables (back then I thought that was easy to do in VB). I figured that I would have to understand the PE-header to make the virus work, but after using some PE-viewing tools (like PE-Explorer), I still didn't understand it.

But I now got enough information about PE-files to make a PE-viewing tool myself. So a year ago I started my UFO-project, just cause PE-Explorer sucks. UFO stands for Ultimate File Optimizer, and it works too, PE-files get smaller when UFO is done with them, and I also added support for other files, like bitmaps. Very cool, but still the PE-files made with VB5 seemed to big. I had to use a hex-editor to delete all that stupid info out of it (like location of the VBP-file). I wanted my UFO-program to delete that stuff, but couldn't find any real documentation. And eventually half a year ago, I got to decompiler.com where I found a little documentation about the structure.

I can't believe you're still reading this, don't you have something better to do? Anyway, I hope you know enough about me now, if not then just ask.

Justin Sane
